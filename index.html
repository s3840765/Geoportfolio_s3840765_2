SIS Analytics

Before transfering to Land Surveying, I was a Geospatial Science student and one of the unit a part of that course what SIS Analytics which delved deeper into how maps can display quantitative data whether through a digital terraim model or a thematic map. For the semester project we had to pose ourself a question or an aim and I decided upon creating a maps that displayed how long it took by bus for students to get to their high school by bus. The scale chosen was just for a city council region, with Whitehorse the chosen area. For this project the main devilerable was a heatmap that displayed the process of how long it took student in certain mesh blocks, the smallest possible area, to get from house to school and this was done in three steps:
  1. Distance and time walking from the house to closest bus stop
  2. Distance and time by bus from the bus stop closest to the house to the bus stop closest to the school
  3. Distance and time walking from the bus stop closest to the school to the school itself

The data was mostly obtained from Data Victoria and all the processing was conducted using the Network Analysis tool in the mapping software Arcmap. This page will briefly cover my work experience (as of OCtober 2023) within land surveying. I have currently only worked for one company being Thinkspatial. This section will describe what i have learned while working in the industry thus far.

Thinkspatial

Undergraduate Surveyor
February 2023 - Present (as of October 2023)
My wok at Thinkspatial has been a good introduction to the Surveying industry as a whole giving me oppourtunities to further my knowledge and experiance in surveying. My roles have been a mixture of both office based work as well as field work in the form of visiting various worksites run by various organisations. Working here has given me knowledge that I wouldn't have aquired at university and it continues to enable me to learn and challenge myself as a surveyor.
